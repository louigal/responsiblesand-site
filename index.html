Sand and silicates: why they matter

Sand and silicates are the most extracted solid materials on Earth. Each year, tens of billions of tonnes are used worldwide to build homes, infrastructure, electronics and energy systems. They have been part of human societies from the beginning — and they still underpin modern life.

Yet aggregates, clays, industrial sands, natural stone and high-purity quartz are often treated as freely abundant and low risk. This is not the case. The scale of consumption, the roles these materials play in ecosystems, and the impacts of some extraction practices make that clear. Even so, sand and silicates sit largely outside the frameworks used to define responsible sourcing and use.

  • Scale — demand is now large enough to shape ecosystems, landscapes and markets.
  • Risk — impacts and rights concerns concentrate in particular contexts and practices.
  • Governance gap — responsibility frameworks have not kept pace with reality.
  • Supply pressure — in some markets, access already affects cost, timing and viability.

When materials are taken for granted, questions of origin, management and accountability fade. The consequences of this invisibility are now clear. Sand and silicate supply chains are linked to human rights, environmental and systemic risks, including growing pressure on secure supply.

Responsible public governance and business conduct are no longer optional. Yet responsibility for sand and silicates remains weakly defined. This creates a rare opening. There is room to lead — to innovate, set credible benchmarks, and open new pathways for value creation and resilience.

Why a Coalition for Responsible Sand and Silicates

We exist to ensure that sand and silicate resources are sourced, used and managed in ways that create value for people and the planet.

Attention to sand and silicates is increasing because of minerals policy, construction decarbonisation and supply-security debates. Standards, knowledge availability, business management tools and innovation must keep pace with this change.

The Coalition is founded on two premises. No single actor can address this shift alone. And those who act early can help shape the path others will follow. We bring together companies, public-good actors and standard-setters to build shared understanding, develop practical guidance and coordinate action.

At its core, our work is about restoring our regard for these materials — reconnecting their management with the systems, places and people they affect.

What we do

The Coalition is a cross-sector platform focused on five actions:

Defining responsibility

Shaping clear norms and guidance, practical tools, and shared red lines and green flags.

Demonstrating solutions

Testing due diligence approaches and innovation practices in real projects and supply chains.

Building shared intelligence

Providing a knowledge hub and making data and insights more actionable.

Aligning existing efforts

Working with civil society, industries, standards bodies and policy processes to identify and fill gaps.

Connecting people

Bringing different groups together to build shared sense-making and coordinate action.

Who we are for

The Coalition is for organisations that rely on sand and silicates — and recognise the need for more responsible ways of working with them.

If you are navigating sourcing risk, due diligence expectations, decarbonisation pressures, biodiversity and social performance challenges, or emerging constraints on supply, we’d like to work with you.

Membership is designed for organisations that want to shape credible practice early — not just follow it later.

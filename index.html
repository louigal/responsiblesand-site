Sand and silicates: why they matter

Sand and silicates are the most extracted solid materials on Earth. They are essential to housing, infrastructure, electronics and energy systems.

These materials — aggregates, clays, industrial sands, natural stone and high-purity quartz — underpin modern life, yet are still widely treated as abundant and low risk.

Because of this, they sit largely outside the frameworks used to define responsible sourcing and use. When materials are taken for granted, questions of origin, impact and accountability fade.

The consequences are now clear. Sand and silicate supply chains are linked to human rights, environmental and systemic risks, including growing pressure on secure supply.

Responsible public governance and business conduct are no longer optional. At the same time, gaps in expectations and practice create a rare opening. Responsibility for sand and silicates is still weakly defined, leaving room to lead — to innovate, set credible benchmarks and open new pathways for value creation and resilience.

Those who move early can help shape the rules others will later follow.

Why a Coalition for Responsible Sand and Silicates

We exist to ensure that sand and silicate resources are sourced, used and managed in ways that create value for people, companies and the planet.

Attention to sand and silicates is increasing across construction decarbonisation, minerals policy and supply-security debates. But expectations, tools and incentives are not keeping pace.

No single actor can address this alone. The Coalition brings together companies, public-good actors and standard-setters to build shared understanding, develop practical guidance and coordinate action.

At its core, our work is about restoring regard — reconnecting materials with the systems, places and people they affect.

What we do

The Coalition is a cross-sector platform focused on four areas:

  • Define responsibility — set clear guidance, practical tools and shared red lines.
  • Demonstrate solutions — test approaches in real projects and supply chains.
  • Build shared intelligence — make data and insight usable.
  • Align existing efforts — work with standards and policy processes to fill gaps.

Who we stand for

The Coalition is for organisations that rely on sand and silicates and recognise the need for more responsible ways of working with them.

We work with:

  • Industry leaders and innovators seeking to improve sourcing, manage risk and shape future expectations, particularly in construction, glass, infrastructure, technology and manufacturing.
  • Civil-society and research organisations contributing expertise on resource governance, biodiversity, social performance, circular economy and community development.
  • Policymakers and regulators developing frameworks for sustainable sourcing and use of mineral-based materials.

We bring these groups together because lasting change depends on shared problem-solving and coordinated action.

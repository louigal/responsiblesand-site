Sand and silicates: why they matter

Sand and silicates are the most extracted solid materials on Earth. Each year, tens of billions of tonnes are used worldwide to build homes, infrastructure, electronics and energy systems. They have been part of human societies from the beginning. And they still underpin modern life.

Aggregates, clays, industrial sands, natural stone and high-purity quartz are often treated as freely abundant and low risk. These assumptions no longer hold.

  • Scale — demand is now large enough to shape ecosystems, landscapes and markets across the world.
  • Risk — environmental, social and human-rights concerns are observed for some extraction and use practices in many different contexts.
  • Governance gaps — responsibility frameworks have not kept pace with reality. Sand and silicates still sit largely outside our practices for responsible minerals sourcing and use. Public governance is often fragmented.
  • Supply pressure — in some markets, access already affects cost, timing and project viability.

When materials are taken for granted, questions of origin, management and accountability fade. The consequences of this invisibility are now clear. Sand and silicate supply chains are linked to human-rights, environmental and systemic risks, including growing pressure on secure supply.

Responsible public governance and business conduct are no longer optional. Yet responsibility for sand and silicates remains weakly defined. This creates a rare opening. There is room to lead — to innovate, set credible benchmarks, and open new pathways for value creation and resilience.

Why a Coalition for Responsible Sand and Silicates

We exist to ensure that sand and silicate resources are sourced, used and managed in ways that create value for people and the planet.

Attention to sand and silicates is increasing — driven by minerals policy, construction decarbonisation and supply-security debates. Expectations are changing. But standards, knowledge, business tools and innovation have not yet caught up with these shifts.

The Coalition is founded on two simple premises.

First, no single actor can address these challenges alone. Responsibility for sand and silicates spans extractive activities, processing, manufacturing, use and reuse — across sectors and jurisdictions.

Second, those who want to act early to help shape what responsibility looks like in practice need a place to do so — with shared evidence, practical support and the conditions to move faster together than they could alone.

At its core, our work is about restoring regard for these materials — reconnecting how they are sourced and managed with the systems, places and people they affect. We see this as an opportunity to build shared understanding, credible guidance and coordinated action on a complex issue.

What we do

The Coalition is a cross-sector platform focused on three actions.

Define and align responsibility

Shape clear norms, practical guidance and usable decision tools — and align existing efforts so gaps are visible and addressed.

Demonstrate solutions in practice

Test due diligence approaches and innovation practices in real projects and supply chains, under real-world conditions.

Build shared intelligence and connection

Provide a knowledge hub, make data and insights more actionable, and convene cross-sector dialogue that supports joint problem-solving and coordinated action.

Who we are for

The Coalition is for organisations that rely on sand and silicates — and recognise the need for more responsible ways of working with them.

If you are navigating sourcing risk, due diligence expectations, decarbonisation pressures, biodiversity and social performance challenges, or emerging constraints on supply, we’d like to work with you.

Membership is designed for organisations that want to shape credible practice early — not just follow it later.

Responsible Sand and Silicates

Building a future where sand and silicates are sourced, used and managed responsibly.

Why do sand and silicates matter?

Sand and silicates are the most extracted solid materials on Earth. Each year, tens of billions of tonnes are used worldwide to build homes, infrastructure, electronics and energy systems. They have been part of human societies from the beginning. And they still underpin modern life.

Aggregates, clays, industrial sands, natural stone and high-purity quartz are often treated as freely abundant and low risk. These assumptions no longer hold.

Scale — demand is now large enough to shape ecosystems, landscapes and markets across the world.

Risk — environmental, social and human-rights concerns are observed for some extraction and use practices in many different contexts.

Governance gaps — responsibility frameworks have not yet caught up with reality. Sand and silicates still sit largely outside our practices for responsible minerals sourcing and use. And public governance is often fragmented.

Supply pressure — in some markets, access already affects cost, timing and project viability.

When materials are taken for granted, questions of origin, management and accountability fade. The consequences of this invisibility are now clear. Sand and silicate supply chains are linked to human-rights, environmental and systemic risks, including growing pressure on secure supply.

Responsible public governance and business conduct are no longer optional. Yet responsibility for sand and silicates remains weakly defined. This creates a rare opening. There is room to lead — to innovate, set credible benchmarks, and open new pathways for value creation and resilience.

Why a Coalition for Responsible Sand and Silicates?

Who we are Why join Get involved

Attention to sand and silicates is increasing — driven by minerals policy, construction decarbonisation and supply-security debates. Expectations are changing. But standards, knowledge, business tools and innovation have yet to catch up.

No single actor can address these challenges alone. And those who want to act early to shape what responsibility looks like in practice need a shared place to do so — one that lets them move faster together.

The Coalition meets these needs, generating new practices, guidance and knowledge that ensures sand and silicates resources are sourced and managed in ways that create value for people and the planet.

What we do

The Coalition is a cross-sector platform focused on three actions.

Defining responsibility

Shaping clear norms and guidance, practical tools and shared red lines and green flags.

Demonstrating solutions

Testing due diligence approaches and innovation practices in real projects and supply chains under real-world conditions.

Building shared intelligence

Providing a knowledge hub and making data and insights actionable, so learning travels beyond individual pilots.

Who we are for

The Coalition is for organisations that rely on sand and silicates and recognise the need for more responsible ways of working with them.

  • Industry leaders and innovators seeking to improve sourcing, manage risk and shape future expectations, particularly in construction, glass, infrastructure, technology and manufacturing.
  • Civil-society and research organisations contributing expertise on resource governance, biodiversity, social performance, circular economy and community development.
  • Policymakers and standards bodies developing frameworks for sustainable sourcing and use of mineral-based materials.

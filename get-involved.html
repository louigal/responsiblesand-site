Who can join

Membership is open to organisations that:

  • have a clear stake in how sand and silicates are sourced, used or governed;
  • support the Coalition’s purpose and values; and
  • are willing to contribute constructively to shared work.

Detailed membership criteria are currently being developed by the Secretariat and will be endorsed by the Council. These criteria will be reviewed and updated over time as the Coalition and its work programme evolve.

Membership model

The Coalition operates a tiered, fee-based membership model, structured by actor type and, where relevant, organisational size.

This reflects how we understand responsibility: as shared, situated and proportionate, rather than outsourced or dominated by any one actor. Sand and silicates connect very different organisations across value chains, geographies and mandates. A tiered model acknowledges those differences openly and practically.

Fee levels for each membership category are set and periodically reviewed, with advice from the multi-stakeholder Council.

Founding Members may choose to make a multi-year commitment, providing early stability and being recognised for that contribution in the Coalition’s public communications.

In addition to annual fees, members may agree specific in-kind contributions — such as staff secondments, hosting meetings or pilot projects, or providing data and technical support — where these align with the agreed work programme.

What we ask of members

Membership comes with shared responsibilities. Members commit to:

  • Compliance with applicable competition and antitrust regulation;
  • A shared code of conduct and data-sharing principles for collaborative work;
  • A minimum level of engagement, including participation in at least one community call per quarter and one substantive peer contribution per year;
  • A short annual progress note or reflection; and
  • Collective problem-solving, including on complex or sensitive issues.

Other ways to partner

In addition to membership, we welcome:

  • Project partnerships — co-developing demonstration projects or targeted research;
  • Secondment arrangements — supporting secretariat functions or founding regional hubs; and
  • Strategic collaborations — with standards bodies, policy processes or initiatives aligned with the Coalition’s mission.

How to start a conversation

  1. Get in touch — share who you are and what you are working on.
  2. Explore fit — discuss alignment and expectations.
  3. Propose a way forward — membership, a project, or a lighter collaboration to start.
